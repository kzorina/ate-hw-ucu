VIII. C ONCLUSION  In this paper we have presented a new paradigm for metric  temporal logics.  We have introduced the simple metric temporal logic  1CMTL which is based on the idea of referring temporal  constraints to the reading on an arbitrarily, but not inÄÅ¹Ânitely  precise single universal clock. This contrasts with existing  metric temporal logics which require speciÄÅ¹Âcations of inÄÅ¹Ânite  accuracy, or at least speciÄÅ¹Âcations which put inÄÅ¹Ânitely accurate  end points on ranges. Existing metric temporal logics also  allow speciÄÅ¹Âcations which are unrealistic to check because  they seem to require the setting off of an inÄÅ¹Ânite number of  inÄÅ¹Ânitely accurate stop-watches.  1CMTL is a general metric temporal logic being able to  handle arbitrary boolean signals over real-numbers time. There  are no ÄÅ¹Ânite variability assumptions on the behaviour of the  signals.  1CMTL is superÄÅ¹Âcially similar to existing MTL but the  semantics are quite different in several ways. Nevertheless,  1CMTL is an expressive language able to specify a wide  range of metric temporal constraints. It captures adequately  approximately all MTL formulas.  We have proved the decidability of 1CMTL by showing  how it it can be translated via the low-level metric MRTL into  the non-metric dense-time temporal logic RTL.
